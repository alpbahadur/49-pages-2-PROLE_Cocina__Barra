Our Story

In April 2019, Chef José Esteban Rivero and his wife Sarah Santos returned to Puerto Rico with a mission—to create more than just a restaurant, but a gathering place rooted in resilience, community, and exceptional cuisine.

After Hurricane Maria, José and Sarah felt called to support their island's recovery. With José's 11 years of culinary training in Philadelphia and Sarah's expertise in marketing and events, they founded PROLE Cocina & Barra in the vibrant Calle Cerra neighborhood of Santurce.

Our name, PROLE, reflects our philosophy: honest, unpretentious, and deeply connected to the land and people of Puerto Rico.

Fresh. Seasonal. Local.

Our commitment to fresh, seasonal ingredients isn't just a concept—it's a promise. We work directly with local Puerto Rican farmers, ensuring every dish celebrates the island's rich agricultural heritage.

Our menu changes every two to three days based on what's freshest and most flavorful, guaranteeing a unique experience with every visit.

Supporting Our Community

Every dish at PROLE tells a story that begins in the fields and farms of Puerto Rico. We work directly with local producers, creating relationships that go beyond transactions.

This commitment means our menu is a true reflection of what's growing right now, supporting the agricultural economy of our island while ensuring unmatched freshness and flavor.

When you dine at PROLE, you're not just enjoying a meal—you're participating in a movement to strengthen Puerto Rico's food system and celebrate its agricultural heritage.
